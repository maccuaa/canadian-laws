# Bill C-16 — Protecting Victims Act

## Summary

This enactment amends various Acts in relation to criminal and correctional matters.
It amends theto, among other things, Criminal Code
The enactment also makes consequential amendments to other Acts.
The enactment also amends theto, among other things, Youth Criminal Justice Act
The enactment also amends theto Canadian Victims Bill of Rights
The enactment also amends theto, among other things, National Defence Act
The enactment also amendsto, among other things, An Act respecting the mandatory reporting of Internet child sexual abuse and exploitation material by persons who provide an Internet service
The enactment also amends theto clarify that an individual whose firearms licence or registration certificate has been revoked is required to deliver their firearm to a peace officer, firearms officer or chief firearms officer and to provide that an individual is not eligible to hold a licence under that Act if the chief firearms officer has reasonable grounds to suspect that the individual may have engaged in an act of domestic violence or stalking. Firearms Act
The enactment also amends theto, among other things, enhance the disclosure of information to victims and other components of the criminal justice system and provide for the submission of victim statements in certain instances. Corrections and Conditional Release Act
Finally, the enactment also amends theto facilitate legal assistance between Canada and supranational bodies with responsibility for criminal investigations or prosecutions. Mutual Legal Assistance in Criminal Matters Act
